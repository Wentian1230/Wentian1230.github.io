Nike Kobe 4


The Nike Kobe 4 Protro stands as a testament to the relentless pursuit of excellence embodied by the late Kobe Bryant, one of the greatest basketball players of all time. This shoe, a modern reinterpretation of the original Kobe 4 released in 2009, carries with it a rich history and a design inspired by Kobe’s unwavering dedication to his craft.

Nike Kobe 5


The Nike Zoom Kobe 5 debuted on Christmas Day in 2009. The Los Angeles Lakers were looking to win another championship in 2009-2010, after bringing home the title a year prior. The Lakers would return to the NBA Finals in 2010. They would defeat the Boston Celtics to bring home back to back championships.

NIke Kobe 6


The original Nike Kobe 6 was a game-changer when it was first released in 2010. Designed by Eric Avar, the Kobe 6 brought the "Black Mamba" moniker to life through its snakeskin-designed upper. The Kobe 6 also continued the shift towards a low-top design that started with the Kobe 4.

Nike Kobe 8

The Kobe 8, a legendary sneaker in the basketball world, made its debut in 2012. Designed by the innovative Eric Avar, the Kobe 8 aimed to evolve the iconic Kobe line by prioritizing thinness and breathability. This focus on performance resonated deeply with basketball enthusiasts, making the Kobe 8 one of the most sought-after sneakers of the 2010s.
